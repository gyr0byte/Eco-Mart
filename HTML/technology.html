Eco-Technology: Innovation Designed Around the Environment

Written by Praful Dhakal

Eco-technology means applying technology in a manner that is not harmful to nature. It is concentrated on the creation of tools and systems, which utilize resources in a wise and environment friendly manner. Most of the eco-technologies are already in our day-to-day lives, and perhaps we do not always realize It.

Energy use is one of the common fields of eco-technology. In technologies such as the solar panels, wind power and small hydro, natural sources like the sun, wind and running water are used to generate electricity. These systems are used in homes, schools, and offices and can work in both villages and towns.

Eco-technology is also useful in the management of natural resources. The use of rainwater collection system, drip irrigation and water storage tanks helps to regulate the way water is gathered and utilized. These are technologies that can be applied in regions where there is scarcity of water and save on unnecessary waste.

In farming, eco-technology supports better planning and decision-making. Weather applications, soil analysis and new irrigation techniques enable farmers to know when to plant and the amount of water to apply. This assists in safeguarding land, crop, and the saving of time and energy.

The eco-technology in cities and towns is witnessed in infrastructure and building designs. Electricity consumption is minimized by using buildings that are natural lit, well ventilated, and use energy saving materials. Homes can be made more comfortable and more affordable through simple design options.

Recycling of the waste materials helps to reduce environment degradation. When a manufacturer launches new version of a product. The older versions are replaced by it. The components of the replaced items can be recycled and used in the manufacturing. This encourages minimal mining.

Waste management is also conducted using eco-technology. Household and community waste is controlled by way of waste sorting systems, recycling tools and composting tools. These mechanisms aim at managing waste in a proper manner rather than throwing everything in a single location.

Another field of application of eco-technology is transportation. EVP, ride sharing, and transportation control applications ensure to plan travel and lessen the fuel consumption. These technologies facilitate ease and efficiency in day to day travel.

Monitoring and information sharing is the other significant component of eco-technology. Environmental sensors include air quality sensors, weather tracking systems, and mapping systems that capture information concerning the environment. The information aids the individual, society and planners to have a better idea of the environmental status.

To sum up, eco-technology is concerned with balance. It is a blend of the contemporary technology and the sensitivity to nature and local requirements. Through simple and practical eco-technologies within the daily lives of the communities, they can strive to pass to a more comfortable future that would be environmentally conscious.
